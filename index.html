From Loud Propaganda to Silent Feeds

Propaganda used to be loud and physical in the DMZ: speakers, leaflets, and balloons crossing a visible border. You could identify the channel, point to the source, and physically remove the object.

Now propaganda is silent and targeted. It arrives through feeds, chat apps, and algorithmic recommendations, customized for specific audiences and pushed at network speed.

You can intercept a balloon. You cannot remove a rumor from the internet once it has been copied, clipped, reposted, and emotionally shared.

The strategic objective is doubt. Not always to convince everyone of one story, but to flood perception until people stop trusting any story at all.

During my service, I learned that the most dangerous weapon is not a gun or artillery, it is Confusion. Now, AI automates confusion.​

According to the 2024 World Economic Forum Global Risk Report, AI-driven disinformation is now considered a top-tier global threat, as it can be used to control narratives and sow division during national crises. WEC

Why Your Eyes Aren't Enough

Sensory Trust

Sensory trust means you believe content because it looks and sounds real in the moment.

It is fast, emotional, and useful in daily life, but easy for synthetic media to exploit.

Verified Trust

Verified trust means belief comes after source checks, provenance signals, and independent confirmation.

It is slower and less exciting, but it survives deliberate manipulation better than instinct does.

Why this matters is simple. Our eyes were never designed to inspect machine-generated intent.

They are good at faces, not at metadata. They are good at emotion, not at origin tracing.

A deepfake is synthetic audio, image, or video generated to imitate a real person or event.

Once deepfakes become ordinary quality, "looks real" stops being a safety check.

In this environment, verification is not paranoia. It is basic digital literacy.

"Do not believe what you can see.
Believe only what you can verify."

From Obvious Fakes to Unverifiable Witnesses

Deepfakes did not only get better. They got harder to verify.

In 2023, synthetic video often looked like a horror meme. The famous "Will Smith eating spaghetti" clip went viral because it looked obviously wrong.

Hands bent unnaturally. Facial features drifted frame to frame. Texture looked unstable. However, the 2026 remake looks far more natural, which means visual giveaways are fading.

Harder verification

The larger threat is not only fake presidents. It is fake ordinary people with no verifiable identity.

No public record. No trusted archive. No clear way to confirm the person exists at all. Even if the clip feels believable, the key question becomes: who is this, and how can we verify them?

So the issue is not "AI equals deception." The issue is unverified distribution plus incentives to manipulate.

Research by the OSCE highlights that state-sponsored disinformation campaigns increasingly leverage AI to manipulate public opinion, specifically by drowning out independent voices and eroding public trust in fact-based reporting. OSCE

Counterpoint: A Balanced View

Not all synthetic media is propaganda.

Public communication: AI presenters can deliver routine updates with clear disclosure.

Accessibility: translation, voice adaptation, and multilingual delivery can widen access.

Creative production: low-cost prototyping helps educators, artists, and small teams publish faster.

The problem is not the existence of the tool. The problem is unverified circulation and deceptive framing.

Good-faith use labels the system. Bad-faith use hides it, then pushes emotional reach before fact-checking.

How to Verify in Real Life

Technology

  • C2PA (Content Credentials): provenance labels that can record origin + edits. C2PA
  • Google SynthID: an invisible watermark that can help identify AI-generated media. SynthID
  • Metadata & context: check earliest upload, account history, and cross-platform consistency.

Culture

  • If content is viral and emotional, pause before sharing.
  • Find the original upload and examine account history.
  • Cross-check with independent reporting before amplification.

Verified trust is slower, but it prevents the high-speed errors manipulation campaigns depend on.

Verification tools aren’t universal yet, and provenance signals can be stripped. So cross-checking sources still matters.

Final Call to Action

In this media environment, confidence can be manufactured.

What protects us is not sharper intuition. What protects us is disciplined verification.

Verified trust is a habit: source → cross-check → provenance → pause.
